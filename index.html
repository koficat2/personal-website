02 — Interactive
Sorting Algorithm Visualizer
An interactive visualization of four fundamental sorting algorithms — Bubble, Selection, Insertion, and Merge sort — rendered frame-by-frame on an HTML Canvas. Each comparison and swap is animated in real time with live counters for total operations. Array size and animation speed are both adjustable. 03 — CS + Statistics
Markov Chain Text Predictor
A browser-based probabilistic language model built entirely from scratch in vanilla JavaScript. Feed it any text corpus — Shakespeare, news articles, your own writing — and it builds an n-th order Markov chain, computing real transition probability distributions across every state. Generates new text by sampling from those distributions with a tunable temperature parameter. Ships with a live transition graph, per-state probability inspector, token frequency chart, and real-time model entropy and perplexity readouts. No libraries. No backend. Pure probability theory applied to language.

04 — Research
Policy Trends in Science Education
Original research using the 2019 Survey of American Science Teachers dataset. Investigated whether a statistically significant relationship exists between a science teacher's political affiliation and their geographic location across U.S. regions. Cleaned and recoded raw survey data down to two variables — political affiliation and state — then aggregated by region using PivotTables and conducted a matched-pairs two-sample t-test in Excel's Data Analysis Toolpak. Built a Tableau choropleth heatmap encoding the Democratic-to-Republican teacher ratio per state, and a stacked bar chart breaking down regional political composition. Concluded with a statistically significant result well below the α = 0.05 threshold, supporting the hypothesis that Democratic-identifying science teachers are disproportionately concentrated in certain regions of the country.
