Top 10 Films of All Time
01
Trois Couleurs: Bleu
Krzysztof Kieślowski, 1993
Grief rendered as pure sensation — Binoche carries the whole film without saying much at all.
02
Scream
Wes Craven, 1996
The rare horror film that's genuinely funny and genuinely scary at the same time.
03
Jagten (The Hunt)
Thomas Vinterberg, 2012
Mads Mikkelsen at his best — a slow-burn about how fast a community can turn on you.
04
Rope
Alfred Hitchcock, 1948
One apartment, one continuous take, more tension than most films with ten times the budget.
05
Dreams
Akira Kurosawa, 1990
Eight dream sequences, each one its own painting — cinema as pure visual poetry.
06
Winter Light
Ingmar Bergman, 1963
Bergman at his most stripped back — a quiet devastation about faith, doubt, and human coldness.
07
Phantom Thread
Paul Thomas Anderson, 2017
A love story disguised as a character study disguised as horror — DDL's best work.
08
Basic Instinct
Paul Verhoeven, 1992
Perfectly calibrated pulp — every scene feels like it's about to go completely off the rails.
09
Mr. & Mrs. Smith
Doug Liman, 2005
Just an insanely fun movie — great chemistry, never takes itself too seriously.
10
Kill Bill
Quentin Tarantino, 2003–04
Style as substance — Tarantino at peak maximalism, a revenge epic that earns every frame.
